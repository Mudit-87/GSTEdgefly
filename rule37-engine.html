Proportionate ITC Reversal Formula (Post Oct 2022 Amendment)
ITC to Reverse = (Unpaid Amount ÷ Invoice Value) × ITC Claimed
Unpaid Amount = Invoice Value − Amount Paid to Supplier
Interest = Reversed ITC × 18% × (Days from ITC Claim to Reversal ÷ 365)
⚠ Interest applies only if ITC was utilised from Electronic Credit Ledger (Rule 88B)

Legal Reference — Rule 37 Key Provisions
When to Reverse
After 180 days from invoice date (not ITC claim date, not goods receipt date). Reverse in the GSTR-3B of the month immediately following the 180-day mark. Report in Table 4(B)(2).
Interest Rate
18% per annum under Sec 50(1). Computed from date of ITC claim to date of reversal. Important: Interest applies ONLY if ITC was utilised from the Electronic Credit Ledger (Rule 88B). If ITC sits unutilised in ECL, no interest.
Re-availment
After reversing and making payment to the supplier, ITC can be re-availed with NO time limit. Report re-availment in GSTR-3B Table 4(D)(1) in the month of payment. No interest on re-availed ITC.
Proportionate Rule
Post Oct 2022 amendment: reverse ITC only on the unpaid proportion. If you paid 60% of invoice, reverse ITC on only the remaining 40%. Formula: (Unpaid ÷ Invoice Value) × ITC Claimed.
RCM Exclusion
Rule 37 does NOT apply to invoices under Reverse Charge Mechanism (Sec 9(3) and 9(4)). Only forward charge invoices where supplier collects and pays GST are covered.
Rule 37A Difference
Rule 37 = buyer's non-payment to supplier within 180 days. Rule 37A = supplier's non-filing of GSTR-3B (different trigger, different engine). Do not confuse the two.

DRC-03 — Invoice-wise Interest Computation
Only invoices where ITC was utilised from ECL — interest @ 18% p.a. from claim date to reversal date
DRC-03 Interest Formula — Rule 88B · Sec 50
Interest = ITC Reversed × 18% × (Days from Claim Date to Reversal Date ÷ 365)
Interest applies ONLY if ITC was utilised from Electronic Credit Ledger (Rule 88B(3))
If ITC balance in ECL was sufficient and not utilised — NIL interest (Rule 88B Explanation I)
⚠ Note: Debate exists whether 18% (Sec 50(1)) or 24% (Sec 50(3)) applies. Safe position: use 18% and disclose basis.

GST Portal Login
Log in at gst.gov.in → Services → Ledgers → Electronic Liability Register → Create DRC-03
2️⃣
Select Reason
Select "Sec 50 interest" as the reason. Select tax period. Enter interest amount from this worksheet.
3️⃣
Pay from ECL / Cash
Interest must be paid in CASH — cannot use ITC balance. Add to Electronic Cash Ledger via challan if insufficient balance.
4️⃣
File & Record
Submit DRC-03. Note the ARN. Come back here and mark as "DRC-03 Filed" against each invoice. Reflects in GSTR-9 Table 9.
